## The Timeline Is Dead

For two decades, video production meant the same thing: import footage, drag clips onto a timeline, scrub frame by frame, export, wait, repeat. Whether you used Premiere Pro, Final Cut, or DaVinci Resolve — the workflow was fundamentally manual.

**Remotion changed the equation entirely.**

Instead of dragging clips on a timeline, you write React components. Instead of keyframing animations by hand, you declare them in code. Instead of rendering one video at a time, you generate thousands programmatically.

## Why Code-Driven Video Wins

The shift from timeline-based editing to code-driven video production isn't incremental — it's categorical. Here's what changes:

### 1. Deterministic Output

Every frame is computed, not approximated. When you write `interpolate(frame, [0, 30], [0, 1])`, you get the exact same result every time. No render variance, no export surprises.

### 2. Infinite Scale

Need 500 personalized product videos for 500 customers? With Remotion, that's a `map()` call over a data array. With traditional editing, that's 500 manual sessions.

### 3. Version Control

Your video is code. It lives in Git. You can diff it, branch it, review it, and roll it back. Try doing that with a `.prproj` file.

### 4. AI Integration

This is where it gets transformative. Claude, GPT, and other LLMs can now **write Remotion code from natural language prompts**. The creative brief becomes the production pipeline.
